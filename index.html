    When any news reaches the desk of an editor, he has to make a few decisions which depends of a number of things, most importantly the size of impact, the importance of the item, recurrence and the audience. Let’s explore them one by one.
    1. The size of impact: If suppose the news is something that has caused immense impact, it will definitely make the cut. Like suppose a train accident with heavy casualties or a militant attack.
    2. Reccurnce: If it’s a recurring event, it tends to loose news space. Like floods in Assam get very small attention because it’s recurring event.
    3. The importance of the news item: How important is the news in terms of National interests is another aspect. The last Assam assembly election was widely covered by the Indian Media not because it has started to give space to NE but because Ground reports suggested that this election might mark the inroads of the BJP into NE.
    4. The Audience: This is the most important factor. And this defines the business of a Media house. So, the editors will have to toe the promoters lines and keep their readers and audiences happy. Republic TV keeps reading news about Pakistan or against the Congress because that is exactly that their viewers want to see. NDTV keeps critisizing Modi.
    The more important reason for N-E not getting attention in media is the majoritarian democracy of India. In a democratic society, where every vote counts, communities with more numerical strength and unity find their voice heard whereas communities with fractured opinion and less numerical strength often find it difficult to get heard. N-E is less populous than the states in Gangetic plains like Bihar and Utter Pradesh thus have less representation inparliament which means lesser say in decision-making at the centre. The another reason is the economic and territorial isolation of N-E from mainland India. The well know chicken neck corridor is the only strip of land that connects India with its North Eastern states.
    People from Northeast are not satisfied with the way media covers them. They believe that Northeast is only covered during insurgencies and very less importance is given to cultural, social and developmental category. When compared to even other trouble ridden states or regions the coverage given, ranks miserably lower. Follow-up stories never crop up and positive reporting like the stories on the beauty of Kashmir never arises. However, the reach of media even during insurgencies is very late.

    The Naga-Manipuri embroil related to the entry of NSCN (1M) Gen Secy Muivah into Manipur resulting in the ongoing blockade of NH-39 lasted for about two months. This economic blockade had a delayed appearance in the mainstream media. Had the media reported on it earlier this blockade would not have lasted two months. The question that arises here is, had this kind of blockade happened in other regions of India would it have lasted for so long and would the media be lethargic to report that too? In fact, research shows that such kind of blockades for example the Telangana hunger strike by the TRS party was instantaneously reported by the media and continued to do so till it ended. This level of reporting forced the central government to step in and constitute a committee to look into the demands of the activists as well as resolve the matter within a month. Most national newspapers have local editions but here again the difference is immense. Local news covers just 2-3 pages of the edition. The rest of the edition is from across the country on various topics such as political, cultural or crime. Here again news from Northeast is a rarity. Consistently greater coverage to other regions is prevalent.
    A case in point stated by the writer is Manipur which has seen many a new developments and incidents in recent times but has still been unable to garner any space on the national media. Fashion shows, companies announcing their quarterly and annual results, job placements in the llMs, cricket matches, Bollywood films and film stars’ romances are regular stories on the national media. In fact, these incidents as brought forth by the writer are happening on a regular basis but the media seems to be content in covering the same repeatedly without bringing forth any new angles to a story.
    In reality the trend in media is quite disturbing. While every hunger protest for Telangana gets mass coverage till the matter is resolved, not many know of from Chanu Shramila or the activists in Sikkim who keep on fighting peacefully for the government to take note of their reservation on the hydel power project at Lepcha reserve of Dzongu. In the absence of interest on part of the mainstream media, people from Northeast India rely on the Internet to share and learn about their states. Online forums, social networking, blogs are the main platforms that are used. Apart from these, talent shows on television like reality shows, Cultural programs,Northeast fests in various cities, Northeast handicraft exhibitions, sports, theatre and art shows are used by the people of Northeast to create awareness about their states.
    One phenomenon that was observed that, the Northeast people wrote 'we' or 'us' when they addressed themselves and they wrote 'them' or 'they' for rest of the people from India. This may also indicate that the Northeast people think of fellow Indians as others and not one among them. This may not only be because of their physical features but also depicts the level of importance they are given in the country. Thus leading to alienation and also raising the potentiality for the birth of identity crises. Indian media from its very roots has always been actively involved and in instances has been made to involve as a platform for not just providing information to the public but also as a forum for making the voice of the public reach those in power. Be it for the 200 years of colonialism or the long standing aspiration of being a developed country to that of the geographic diversities and distance, media in India has served as a unifying force as well as an equalizer of power. Today media is considered to be one of the most powerful weapons that can bring drastic changes in the economy of the country, only if used in a proper way. It is time that media should contribute to the development of Northeast region, educate the common people, widening their thoughts and show that even the Northeastern states are very much a part of India.
    I do believe the situation is changing rapidly, and that we can expect the North-Easteaners to truly be seen as fellow countrymen who are actually doing right what the rest of India is doing wrong (women empowerment, egalitarian casteless society, etc).
